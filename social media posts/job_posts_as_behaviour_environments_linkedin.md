# Job posts as behavioural environments

The more I look at job posts, the more they look like a behavioural design problem.

Job posts are behavioural environments. Every step, every form field, every instruction is a behavioural intervention that nudges people to continue or quit. And yet, they're still being treated as 'just admin'.

I've covered this in a previous post, but to reiterate, friction and slow responses don't filter for the things that we often assume they filter for.

You think you're seeing candidate motivation, commitment, and patience but the system is actually selecting for people with fewer options and a willingness to tolerate having their time wasted.

This is the space I'm becoming increasingly interested in: job posts as applied behavioural design.

I'm interested in where friction shapes candidate behaviour. Where candidates drop out of the process, what else the job post is selecting for, how we signal what we're looking for without placing a burden on the candidate reading the job post, and overall, how to design the kind of funnel that attracts the people we really want.

Job posts could benefit from being shorter, but they could also benefit from being smarter.
